## Participants

Not listed on the Confluence page. The Batch&Matrix on-call team ran the session. At the time of the retro, the team was a two-person subteam.

## Steps Performed

The retro covered the required on-caller skill set, going through each area:

1. **Overall architecture** - reviewed diagrams at https://github.com/tomtom-internal/batch-service2-infra/tree/master/docs/diagrams
2. **Quota management** - reviewed quota docs (significantly different from a typical sync API):
   - Batch&Matrix 1.2 quotas: https://tomtom.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/DIRECTIONS/pages/233915849
   - Matrix Routing v2 quotas: https://tomtom.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/DIRECTIONS/pages/287900086
3. **Impact assessment** - reviewed dashboards (quota usage, status codes, executor, health indicators), Scalyr & [Loki](https://grafana.com/docs/loki/latest/) logs, and graceful degradation scenarios:
   - Classic Batch&Matrix may be down while Matrix v2 still works.
   - Matrix v2 async may be down while sync still works.
4. **Region turn-off procedure** - reviewed runbook at https://tomtom.atlassian.net/wiki/spaces/DIRECTIONS/pages/233927941. Needed approximately once every two years.
5. **Immediate actions** - component rollout restart pipelines:
   - Batch cluster rollout restart: https://ci.dev.batch.tt4.nl/view/Batch/view/Prod/view/Batch/job/rollout_restart_batch_cluster_prod/
   - [Pulsar](https://pulsar.apache.org/docs/) cluster rollout restart: https://ci.dev.batch.tt4.nl/view/Batch/view/Prod/view/Pulsar/job/rollout_restart_pulsar_cluster_prod/
6. **[PIM](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/entra/id-governance/privileged-identity-management/pim-configure) access** - jumphost and [kubectl](https://kubernetes.io/docs/reference/kubectl/) access for checking cluster and pod status.
7. **P1 to Microsoft** - how to escalate infra issues to Microsoft (e.g., Azure Storage failure with no replacement available and [Azure Traffic Manager](https://learn.microsoft.com/en-us/azure/traffic-manager/traffic-manager-overview) cannot be disabled).
8. **Deployment of an older version** if a rollback is necessary.
9. **Apigee/Gloo integration awareness** - low occurrence risk; APIM changes typically happen during business hours with CAB process, unlikely to cause night-time incidents.

## Issues Encountered

- The team operates as a two-person subteam — an unfavorable situation the organizer is working to improve.
- The region turn-off procedure needs improvement: a simple pipeline would make it easier to execute.
- Training on complex topics is hard to organize; the session format needs improvement.

## Lessons Learned

- Batch&Matrix stability relies on thorough documentation, diagrams, and alert runlinks. Alerts link directly to relevant dashboards and docs, so on-callers don't need to memorize many things.
- Key docs: https://github.com/tomtom-internal/batch-service2-infra/tree/master/docs
- Quota management is non-obvious and differs significantly from standard sync APIs — study it separately.
- Know graceful degradation: the system has multiple independent layers (classic vs. v2, async vs. sync) that can fail independently.
- Know the Apigee/Gloo integration even though off-hours disruptions are unlikely.

## Action Items

- Build a simple pipeline to automate the region turn-off procedure (currently manual, needed ~once every two years).
- Grow the on-call rotation beyond two people to reduce individual risk.
- Improve on-call training materials and drill formats to make complex scenarios more approachable.
